<p>For nearly two decades, organizations across healthcare, drug discovery, and financial services have relied on SAS Grid to power their most critical analytics workloads. SAS Grid (formally known as SAS Grid Manager) is an enterprise software platform developed by SAS Institute that distributes and manages large-scale analytical workloads across a cluster of servers. It acts as a job scheduler and resource manager that enables parallel processing, load balancing, and high availability. Think of it as an air traffic controller at a busy airport: instead of all planes (jobs) trying to land on one runway (server), it directs them across multiple runways to keep everything moving smoothly. For industries such as healthcare, drug discovery, and financial services, SAS Grid has been the backbone of enterprise analytics, powering complex statistical models, clinical trial analyses, risk modeling, and fraud detection at massive scale.</p> 
<p>Today, storage innovations from <a href="https://aws.amazon.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Amazon Web Services (AWS)</a> are making it more cost-effective than ever to migrate these legacy SAS Grid environments to the cloud—without sacrificing the performance required by these demanding applications. Traditionally, SAS Grid runs on expensive on-premises hardware and moving it to the cloud raises real concerns about slowdowns and reliability. AWS directly bridges that gap, delivering the flexibility and economics of the cloud while preserving the speed and resilience on which these workloads depend.</p> 
<p>This post is written for anyone involved in deciding, building, or paying for cloud infrastructure in SAS Grid-heavy organizations. This includes admins and architects doing technical planning, IT leaders in regulated industries evaluating options, data scientists concerned about performance impacts, and finance teams focused on cost optimization.</p> 
<h3>The on-premises challenge</h3> 
<p>Managing clustered computing and storage environments for SAS Grid requires substantial hardware investment and specialized expertise that many customers would rather not maintain. On-premises SAS Grid deployments demand expensive storage arrays, often combining hard disk drives (HDD) and solid-state drives (SSD) to balance performance and cost. As datasets grow into the petabyte range, organizations face continuous capital expenditures to expand capacity and maintain performance.</p> 
<p>Beyond the hardware costs, running SAS Grid on-premises requires dedicated teams to manage storage provisioning, capacity planning, disaster recovery, and infrastructure maintenance. These operational burdens divert resources from innovation and strategic initiatives.</p> 
<p>Moving to the cloud seems like the obvious answer—and it is. However, until now storage options for high-performance workloads such as SAS Grid have been limited to more expensive all-SSD solutions. Although these deliver excellent performance, they come at a premium price point that can make cloud migration economically challenging, especially for organizations with multi-petabyte datasets that include both frequently and infrequently accessed data.</p>

This innovative storage solution is specifically designed for workloads such as SAS Grid that have a mix of hot and cold data and don’t require consistent all-SSD performance for every byte of every file.</p> 
<p>FSx for Lustre Intelligent-Tiering offers the lowest-cost high-performance file storage in the cloud, reducing storage costs for infrequently accessed data by up to 96% when compared to other managed Lustre options. <h3>How Amazon FSx Intelligent-Tiering works</h3> 
<p>The value of FSx Intelligent-Tiering lies in its automatic data management. Your data moves seamlessly between three storage tiers based on access patterns, with no manual intervention required:&nbsp;</p> 
<p><em><strong>1) Frequent Access tier:</strong></em> Data accessed within the last 30 days stays in this tier.</p> 
<p><em><strong>2) Infrequent Access tier:</strong></em> Data not accessed for 30–90 days automatically moves here, reducing costs by 44% when compared to the Frequent Access tier.</p> 
<p><em><strong>3) Archive tier:</strong></em> Data untouched for 90+ days migrates to this tier, delivering a 65% cost reduction when compared to the Infrequent Access tier.</p> 
<p>Regardless of which tier your data resides in, it remains instantly accessible with retrieval times measured in tens of milliseconds—not hours or days. This means that your SAS Grid applications continue to run without modification, transparently accessing data across all tiers as needed. Current analysis datasets remain in high-performance storage, historical datasets move to lower-cost storage, reference data is automatically tiered based on usage, and seasonal or cyclical workloads benefit from dynamic tiering.</p> 
<h3>Fully elastic storage that scales with your needs</h3> 
<p>Unlike traditional storage systems that require upfront capacity provisioning, FSx for Lustre Intelligent-Tiering is fully elastic. Your filesystem is virtually unlimited in space and you only pay for the data that you store. This eliminates the expensive over-provisioning that’s common with on-premises storage arrays, where organizations must purchase capacity for future growth to avoid running out of space.</p> 
<p>For SAS Grid workloads, this elasticity is transformative. You can start with gigabytes of experimental data and seamlessly scale to petabyte-scale production datasets without any storage management overhead or capacity planning exercises.</p>
